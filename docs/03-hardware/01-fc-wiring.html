Section 03 · Step 01 · ~20 min

Wire the flight controller.

If your platform from class was a quad, fixed-wing, rover, or boat, your Jetson talks to a Pixhawk flight controller over a serial connection. This page covers the wiring. The MAVLink test is on the next page. The Pixhawk's own ArduPilot parameters were configured in class and are not covered here.

Skip Skip this whole section if you do not have a Pixhawk, or if your Jetson is purely a compute payload (Ollama, ComfyUI, no vehicle).

Three wires, that's it

For TELEM2 UART you wire three jumpers from the Jetson's 40-pin GPIO header to the Pixhawk's TELEM2 port. The other three pins of the JST-GH cable are unused for this. The diagram below shows the full picture.

Trap Never power Pixhawk from USB and battery at the same time.

Both paths can damage the Pixhawk. Do NOT power the Pixhawk from both USB and the vehicle battery at the same time. USB power backfeeds the Pixhawk's voltage rails and can damage them. For bench work: power the Pixhawk only via USB (motors will not spin without battery, which is what you want). For flight: battery only, no USB.

Two connection options

You will use one of these depending on the situation:

OptionWhen to useConnectorSpeed
USB-C Bench testing, fault-finding, no flight USB-C cable, Pixhawk to Jetson 115200 baud (virtual serial)
TELEM2 UART Field deployment, on-vehicle 6-pin JST-GH cable + 3 dupont jumpers 921600 baud

USB-C is the easy path. Plug a USB-C cable from the Pixhawk to a Jetson USB port, the Pixhawk shows up as /dev/ttyACM0. Done. Use this for first-time testing.

TELEM2 UART is the field path. You cannot keep a USB-C cable dangling on a vehicle, so for real flights or runs you wire 3 jumpers from the Jetson's 40-pin GPIO header to the Pixhawk's TELEM2 port. This is what the rest of this page covers.

TELEM2 UART pin map

Jetson 40-pin headerPixhawk TELEM2 (JST-GH 6-pin)What it does
Pin 8 (UART1 TX) Pin 3 (RX, Pixhawk's input) Jetson talks to Pixhawk
Pin 10 (UART1 RX) Pin 2 (TX, Pixhawk's output) Pixhawk talks to Jetson
Pin 6 (GND) Pin 6 (GND) Common ground (required)

Important: TX always crosses to RX. Jetson TX (pin 8) goes to Pixhawk RX (pin 3). If you wire TX-to-TX, nothing works and there is no error message. It just silently does not connect.

The Pixhawk side labels (TX, RX) are from the Pixhawk's perspective. So Pixhawk's "TX" is its output, which goes to your input (Jetson RX). Easy to flip if you read them as Jetson-side labels.

Logic levels, both 3.3V

Note The Jetson 40-pin GPIO header runs at 3.3V logic. The Pixhawk TELEM2 also runs at 3.3V logic. They wire directly together with no level shifter required. You may see other docs claiming the Jetson UART is 1.8V; that is a different UART (the debug UART), not the one on the 40-pin header. The pin 8/10 UART that this page uses is 3.3V and tested working with no level shifter (heartbeat received in under 2 seconds, multiple field sessions). If you wire it and get nothing, the cause is almost always TX/RX swapped or a missing GND, not a logic-level mismatch.

Cable options

You have a few choices for the actual physical cable:

  1. The cable that came with your Pixhawk. Most Pixhawk 6C kits ship with a 6-pin JST-GH-to-loose-pin "telemetry" cable. Trim and strip the 3 wires you need (TX, RX, GND), terminate them with female dupont connectors, plug them onto the Jetson header.
  2. JST-GH-to-Dupont harness from the Holybro store. Pre-terminated, prettiest option.
  3. Build your own with a JST-GH crimp kit if you have one.

Do not try to solder directly to the Jetson header pins. The pins are short and the carrier board is delicate.

Identifying the right pins

The Jetson 40-pin header is right next to the heatsink. Pin 1 is at the corner closest to the SD card slot, marked with a tiny silkscreen "1" or a square pad (the rest are round).

Counting from pin 1:

  • Pin 6 = GND (3rd row, side closest to the board edge)
  • Pin 8 = UART1 TX (4th row, side closest to the board edge)
  • Pin 10 = UART1 RX (5th row, side closest to the board edge)

If you put pin 1 in the wrong corner, you will wire to pins 33, 35, 37 by accident. If MAVLink does not connect, double-check pin 1 orientation before debugging anything else.
