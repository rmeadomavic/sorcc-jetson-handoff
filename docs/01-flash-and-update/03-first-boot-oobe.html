First-boot wizard

Ubuntu's out-of-box experience wizard runs the first time you boot any fresh JetPack image. It is a series of screens asking about language, network, account, and so on. None of it is hard, but a couple of choices matter.

Walk through the wizard

License agreement

Click I Accept.

Language

English (United States), or whatever you actually want. Click Continue.

Keyboard layout

English (US), match your physical keyboard. Click Continue.

Wi-Fi

Pick your network and enter the password. Required. The Jetson needs internet for the post-setup updates. If you have Ethernet plugged in, you can pick I don't want to connect to a Wi-Fi network right now and use Ethernet instead.

Time zone

Pick yours. Click Continue.

User account

FieldWhat to put
Your namesorcc
Your computer's namejetson (or whatever; this is the hostname)
Pick a usernamesorcc
Choose a passwordsorcc
Confirm passwordsorcc
Log in automaticallyCheck this box

Click Continue.

Why sorcc / sorcc? Matches the convention from class. Everything in 04-apps/ assumes the username is sorcc. If you use something else, you will need to mentally substitute throughout. For the field: once you are operating the Jetson on a real platform, change to a stronger password with passwd. The default is fine for bench and lab.

Partition size

Accept the default (use the whole SD card). Click Continue.

Chromium browser

Click Install. You will need a browser later to access Hydra's web dashboard. Wait for it to finish, then click Close.

The Jetson will apply the settings and reboot automatically. Wait through this. It can take 5 minutes.

Post-reboot popups

After the reboot you land on the Ubuntu desktop. A few popups will appear over the next minute. Handle them in this order.

Online accounts

Click Skip. You do not need a Google or Microsoft account for this.

Ubuntu Pro

Click Skip. Ubuntu Pro is a paid Canonical thing you do not need.

Send system info to Canonical

Pick No, don't send system info. Click Next.

Location services

Heads up Leave Location Services OFF.

The Jetson does not need OS-level location services. If you are using Hydra Detect, it gets GPS from the flight controller via MAVLink, which is far better than the Jetson's nonexistent GPS hardware. Ubuntu's location services would just sit there making background network calls for nothing.

Click Next.

Done

Click Done.

System updater popup

Within a couple of minutes, a Software Updater window pops up. Click Install Now, enter your password (sorcc), and let it run.

Heads up This takes 20 to 30 minutes. Get coffee. Do not power off.

When it finishes, click Restart Now.

Trap Do NOT power off during the post-update reboot.

During restart you will see an NVIDIA boot screen with an update progress bar. The Jetson is applying kernel and firmware updates. Cutting power here can leave the system in a bad state. Wait for the desktop to come back up.

When the desktop comes back up, you are ready for Step 04.

Sanity check

Open a terminal (Ctrl+Alt+T) and run:

Shell · sorcc@jetson 
$ cat /etc/nv_tegra_release
# Expected first line: # R36 (release), REVISION: 4.x, GCID: ..., ...

$ docker --version
# Expected: Docker version 27.x or newer

$ df -h /
# Should show your SD card with most of it as free space.

If you see R36, JetPack 6 is alive. You are good. Onwards.
