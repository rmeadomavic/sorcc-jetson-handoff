Section 01 · Step 02 · ~20 min

Flash JetPack 6.x

You have done the QSPI update. Now you flash your real, permanent operating system to a microSD card and boot it.

What you need

  • A microSD card, 64 GB+ recommended, UHS-I (Class 10) or faster
  • Etcher on your host PC (Windows, Mac, or Linux)
  • The JetPack 6.x SD card image, see below

Get the JetPack 6.x image

  1. Go to developer.nvidia.com/embedded/downloads.
  2. Find "JetPack 6.2.1 SD Card Image, Jetson Orin Nano Developer Kit", or whatever the latest 6.x is when you read this.
  3. Download. The file is about 10 GB and ends in .img.xz.
About card quality The cheapest microSD cards on Amazon are often counterfeit and will fail in weird, intermittent ways. Stick to SanDisk Extreme, Samsung EVO Select, or Kingston Canvas Go from a real retailer. A 64 GB card is fine; 128 GB gives you breathing room for ML models.

The procedure

01

Flash the card.

  1. Plug the SD card into your host PC's reader.
  2. Open Etcher.
  3. Flash from file, pick the JP 6.x .img.xz you downloaded.
  4. Select target, pick your SD card. Etcher will not let you pick your system drive, but double-check the size matches your SD card.
  5. Flash. This takes 10 to 15 minutes depending on card speed.
  6. When Etcher finishes, it runs a verify pass automatically. Do not skip verify. A failed verify means a bad flash and you will save yourself hours by re-flashing now.
02

Boot it.

  1. Eject the SD card from your host PC.
  2. Insert it into the Jetson's microSD slot.
  3. Plug in monitor, keyboard, mouse, USB-C power.
  4. Power on.

You should see the NVIDIA logo, then a Linux boot sequence, then the Ubuntu first-boot wizard.

What you might see instead

  • If you see nothing, black screen for more than 2 minutes, your QSPI update probably did not take. Go back to Step 01 troubleshooting.
  • If you see Linux boot text but it kernel-panics or hangs, your SD card flash is bad. Re-flash with verify enabled.
  • If you see the Ubuntu wizard, congratulations, JetPack 6 is alive. Continue to Step 03.
